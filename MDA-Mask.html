Abstract

In the field of image generation, precisely controlling the generated content to align with user-specified personalized concepts while maintaining natural integration with general semantic scenes remains a key challenge. Previous methods struggle with two critical technical bottlenecks when addressing the issue of missing general concepts during generation with custom concepts: 1) over-constraining custom concepts leads to custom concepts generalization, and 2) background regions interfere with the encoding of custom concepts. We propose MDA-Mask, a novel personalized image generation framework that incorporates a Maximal Divergent Anchoring (MDA) constraint and a background leakage suppression (BLS) mechanism. Our approach leverages GPT-4o to select multiple general concepts and identifies three concepts with the largest semantic gap as anchor concepts to constrain custom concepts, ensuring they maintain a distinct distance from general concepts. Additionally, we apply a BLS mechanism to reduce background interference in encoding. We assess performance by combining multiple specific concepts with various general concepts. Compared to mainstream methods, our framework improves FID, text alignment, and image alignment metrics by 4.3%, 5.2%, and 3.1%, respectively, markedly enhancing concept composability.
